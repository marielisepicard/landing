Launching a startup: Pianity

Last week, Sydney shared his experience of YCombinator. Today, we asked Kevin to share about Pianity, his new project.

What is Pianity?

 

Pianity is where musicians and fans gather to create, collect and trade unique digital artworks. In other words, Pianity is a marketplace where musicians transform their track into non-fungible tokens (NFTs).

Until today musicians were in an odd position because they did not have scarcity. When a painter creates paintings it is unique by essence: The is only one Mona Lisa. However, when a musician creates a song, it can be infinitely replicated.

For the first time, NFTs allow to bring scarcity (and true ownership) to the music industry.

 

How did you start / had the idea?

My co-founder is a musician. We were at a party when I jokingly told him: ‚Äúwe should put your tracks on the blockchain!‚Äù. At first, we saw that as a new way of proof of copyright. The idea later evolved into a new business model for the music industry as a whole.

 

Tell us more about the recent release

We released Pianity less than a month after we had the idea (30th of March). It is a fully functional marketplace where musicians can mint their own NFTs and sell them through auctions. We released 8 NFTs so far, most of them were sold for 200+ euros.

 

What is your main focus for the next week / your next goal?

Our main focus right now is distribution, i.e. reaching as many fans (collectors) as possible. The magic about Pianity is artists are bringing the fan on the platform for us. Therefore we focus our acquisition on artists.

    From Big Mama to Sunday

    If you're not French, especially not Parisian, you may not know Big Mamma. But we don't know any Parisian that doesn't know (and love) its amazing (and affordable) Italian food that Parisians used to discover among their dozen fashion restaurants.

    But today, it's not about mozzarella, it's about Sunday, a new startup made by the same founder, Victor Lugger, Tigrane Seydoux and Christine de Wender (Christine is not from Big Mamma but from Zalando and ManoMano). What is Sunday ? it's a startup aiming at facilitate the payment in restaurants. Thanks to a QR code, clients can pay by themself directly from their phone.

    Sunday just raised $24 million seed round, for a $140 million post-money valuation. It's already impressive and if you're interested, they're hiring developers.
